## Common Issues Identified

### 1. Vague/Unsourced Metrics
Many case studies claim specific percentages (15-20%, 30%, etc.) without any verifiable source. These numbers appear fabricated or copied from unrelated studies.

### 2. Irrelevant References
References frequently cite:
- Generic "Top 100 Quantum Companies" listicles
- Academic papers about quantum computing generally (not about the specific project)
- Company homepages
- Paywalled reports

### 3. AI-Generated Filler Phrases
Recurring patterns across low-scoring content:
- "significant step/milestone"
- "positioned as a leader"
- "valuable intellectual property"
- "transformative potential"
- "cutting-edge technology"

### 4. "Results Confidential/Proprietary"
Several case studies (PayPal, Saudi Aramco) hide behind confidentiality, making them essentially useless as case studies.

### 5. Future-Heavy Results
Many "Results" sections pivot immediately to future plans rather than documenting actual achievements.

### 6. Partnership Announcements Treated as Case Studies
Some entries (Toyota Ventures, Xanadu-Haiqu) are investment/partnership announcements with no technical implementation.

## Recommended Action Plan

### Phase 1: Immediate (Bottom 6)
Remove or completely rewrite the 6 lowest-scoring case studies. These damage credibility.

### Phase 2: Priority Rework (Scores 12-15)
Research and rewrite the 20 case studies scoring 12-15. Focus on:
- Finding primary sources (press releases, papers, interviews)
- Adding concrete metrics or honestly stating none exist
- Removing filler phrases
- Shortening "Future Directions" sections

### Phase 3: Polish (Scores 16-23)
Minor improvements to acceptable case studies:
- Add/fix reference URLs
- Verify claimed metrics
- Tighten prose

### Phase 4: Maintain (Scores 24-30)
The 3 flagship case studies need minimal work. Use them as templates.
